# NullRoute: Emergency Removal Guide

Because this application uses an **Accessibility Service** designed to block access to your phone's System Settings (preventing you from stopping or disabling the VPN blocker), normal uninstallation is restricted. 

If you ever need to uninstall the app in an emergency (e.g., if a system update breaks the VPN or you need immediate access to a blocked site for an emergency), follow these steps to bypass the lock using **Android Safe Mode**.

---

## What is Android Safe Mode?
Safe Mode boots your Android device with only the default pre-installed system apps active. All third-party apps, including **NullRoute** and its background/accessibility services, are **temporarily disabled**. This allows you to safely access your settings and uninstall the app.

---

## Step-by-Step Bypass / Removal Procedure

### Step 1: Boot into Safe Mode
Methods vary slightly depending on your phone's manufacturer, but the standard method is:

1. **If the phone is ON:**
   * Press and hold the physical **Power** button until the power menu options (Power Off, Restart, etc.) appear on the screen.
   * Tap and **hold** the **"Power Off"** or **"Restart"** option on the screen for 2–3 seconds.
   * A prompt should appear asking if you want to reboot to **Safe Mode**. Tap **OK** or **Confirm**.
2. **If the phone is OFF (Alternative):**
   * Turn the phone on.
   * As soon as the manufacturer logo appears on the screen, press and **hold the Volume Down** button.
   * Keep holding it until the phone finishes booting.
3. **Verification:**
   * You will see the words **"Safe Mode"** displayed in the bottom-left corner of your screen.

---

### Step 2: Disable Accessibility & Uninstall the App
Now that you are in Safe Mode, NullRoute is not running and cannot block your actions.

1. Open your phone's **Settings** app.
2. Go to **Apps** (or **Apps & Notifications**) -> **See all apps**.
3. Scroll down and find **NullRoute**.
4. Tap **Uninstall** and confirm.
5. *(Optional)* Alternatively, if you just want to turn off the block temporarily, you can navigate to **Settings** -> **Accessibility** -> **NullRoute** and toggle it **OFF**.

---

### Step 3: Reboot Back to Normal Mode
1. Simply restart your phone normally (press the Power button and select **Restart**).
2. The phone will boot normally, and the blocker will be completely gone or disabled.
